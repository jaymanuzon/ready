AI in your life · Self-check

What this is about

“Am I missing out on AI right now?” — almost everyone is asking themselves that question at the moment. This check turns it into an organised view: across five areas of life, it shows where AI could already give you time and clarity today, and where your next simple step lies. At the end you get an overall picture, a heatmap for each area and your personal levers — instantly, no waiting.

Purpose

An orientation, not a report card. A first, honest look at where AI can make your life easier.

Duration

About 10 minutes. 30 statements, each on a scale from 1 to 5.

Who it's for

For you personally — whether you use AI daily or have only heard about it so far. There are no wrong answers.

What this test deliberately can't do — and that's intentional. It measures how you see yourself, not how things objectively are. It doesn't sell you anything and it doesn't judge you — it sorts. What you make of it is up to you. At the end we show you one possible next step; nothing more.

How to answer

You rate each statement on a scale from 1 (doesn't apply at all) to 5 (fully applies). If you can't judge it, that's a 1 — and that's an honest answer too.

1Doesn't apply at all / I don't know
2Mostly doesn't apply
3Partly applies
4Mostly applies
5Fully applies / well established

Statements marked reverse-scored are deliberately worded the other way round: there, strong agreement means untapped potential. They are converted automatically in the assessment (6 − value) — no need to think backwards, just answer honestly.

    What this self-check deliberately can't do

    These limits aren't a weakness to be optimised away — they're what keeps the test honest:

    • Self-assessment is not an outside view. The test measures how you see yourself — and even that is valuable, because it sorts things out.
    • It tells you what, not how. You get the first step for each area. How it fits into your everyday life, only you know — or you work it out together with a companion.
    • No judgement about you. The check is an anchor for reflection, never a verdict. A low number only means: something is still waiting there for you.
